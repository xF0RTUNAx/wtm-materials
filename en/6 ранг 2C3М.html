Rank 6 2S3M

Historical Background

The 2S3M "Akatsiya" is an upgraded version of the 2S3 self-propelled howitzer. On the modernized variant, a single drum-type mechanized ammunition rack was installed instead of the two on the base model, increasing the carried ammunition from 40 to 46 rounds. Additionally, the configuration and location of hatches in the rear part of the hull and turret were changed, a mechanized supply of rounds from the ground was introduced, the radio antenna was moved to the turret roof, and the R-123 radio was replaced with the R-123M. The 2S3M entered serial production in 1975. it was in service with the USSR and Iraq, as well as other post-Soviet countries. The "Akatsiya" was first used in the Afghan War. It also participated in many other conflicts, including the Iran-Iraq War.

Quick Overview

Maneuverability

Pros: The maneuverability of this artillery piece is sufficient for most natural tasks. Even the reverse speed, compared to standard Soviet tanks, is adequate for routine situations.

Cons: The main drawbacks are the turret rotation speed and the speeds of vertical and horizontal guidance. This is crucial to consider in battles, and using hull traverse to assist aiming is recommended where appropriate.

Armor and Survivability

In practice, protection is often non-existent. This forces a more cautious playstyle, as the vehicle is vulnerable to damage from almost all cannons. The "Akatsiya" can sometimes survive hits due to the dispersed modules and damage absorption by the engine and transmission. Do not forget about the ammunition placement in the rear of the hull and turret. Additionally, the right side of the hull and turret carries the most significance, which is important when playing as or against the Akatsiya.

Armament

As with most vehicles of this class, the essence lies in its firepower. Despite an impressive range of ammunition, it is recommended to use the top-tier High Explosive (HE) shells. The amount of explosive filler is sufficient to create overpressure and defeat even well-armored vehicles via weak spots. Radio-fused shells are only useful for engaging aircraft. It is vital not to forget the truly long reload time, especially if reload skill is not upgraded. Furthermore, the loss of crew members increases this value. Another downside is the poor negative vertical guidance (gun depression). Conversely, the upward elevation is enough to target planes.

Features

Keep in mind that due to the rear-mounted turret, peaking with the front of the hull often leads to immobilization and gradual destruction through fires.

Conclusions and Comments

The "Akatsiya" is a very pleasant and effective machine. It is definitely recommended for those who enjoy playing artillery, especially at lower ranks. It takes some time to get used to the vehicle and its shell ballistics, but it won't disappoint afterwards. Certainly, this class of vehicle is demanding of appropriate battle conditions for proper effectiveness.
