7 rank Object 906

Historical Background

The Object 906 (PT-85) is a Soviet experimental light tank developed by STZ (later VgTZ) engineers in the early 1960s. The main goal of the designers was to create a new amphibious tank similar to the PT-76, but with greater firepower.

In this regard, it was decided to equip the new PT-85 with an 85-mm D-58 gun with a loading mechanism. In 1961–1962, 6 prototypes were manufactured, two of which were tested in 1963. The Object 906 was not adopted for service.

Brief Overview

Maneuverability

Pros:

  • High speed: Quickly reaches its maximum speed, which allows it to take key positions.
  • Excellent maneuverability: Handles and turns well at speed.
  • Reverse speed: Fast retreat (reverse speed is practically equal to forward speed).
  • Amphibious: Capable of crossing water obstacles, expanding tactical options.

Cons:

  • Weak armor: Easily penetrated by cannons and even machine guns, requiring a cautious playstyle.
  • Loss of speed: Reduced dynamics on soft ground or when overcoming obstacles.
  • Dimensions: The tank is quite tall, making it visible on the battlefield.

Armor and Survivability

  • Armor: Bulletproof, aluminum. The hull front can theoretically withstand 12.7-mm machine guns, but the sides and rear are penetrated by almost everything.
  • Survivability: Extremely low. The Object 906 is vulnerable to aircraft, artillery, and heavy machine guns.
  • Crew: 3 members (commander, gunner, driver), two of whom are located in a cramped turret.
  • Protection against hits: APHE, solid shot, and APFSDS shells hitting the side can pass straight through without causing critical damage, but this happens quite rarely.

Armament

  • Gun: 85-mm D-58 tank gun.
  • Autoloader: Mechanized stowage for 15 rounds inside the turret, providing a high rate of fire.
  • Ammunition: 40 rounds.
  • Shell types:
    • BR-372: Armor-piercing high-explosive (APHE) shell (speed up to 1000 m/s, penetration up to 240 mm).
    • ZBK7: High-explosive anti-tank (HEAT) non-rotating shell (speed up to 925 m/s, penetration up to 300 mm).
    • OF-372: High-explosive fragmentation (HE) shell.
  • Machine gun: 7.62-mm SGMT machine gun coaxial with the main gun (ammunition 2000 rounds).
  • Stabilization: Two-plane stabilizer, allowing comfortable firing on the move.

Conclusions and Comments

Due to the almost complete lack of armor, this tank requires an ambush playstyle and high mobility. The survivability of the vehicle directly depends on your attentiveness and ability to change positions in time.

Use the excellent reverse speed for a quick retreat after firing, and the amphibious properties along with the two-plane stabilizer for unexpected flanking maneuvers through water. The 15-round autoloader and powerful HEAT shells will allow you to quickly and effectively dismantle even heavily armored enemy targets.
