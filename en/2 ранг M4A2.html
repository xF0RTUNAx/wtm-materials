Rank 2 M4A2

Historical Background

The M4A2 is a modification of the American Sherman family of medium tanks, distinguished by the installation of a General Motors diesel power plant. Unlike the gasoline versions, the M4A2 was supplied primarily under Lend-Lease and was actively used by the armies of the USSR, Great Britain, and other allies.

The tank was valued for its reliability, stable engine performance, and overall ease of maintenance. Based on its combined characteristics, the M4A2 became one of the most widespread and recognizable versions of the Sherman during World War II.

In War Thunder Mobile, the M4A2 maintains its historical image as a versatile medium tank with comfortable armament and balanced characteristics.

Quick Overview

Maneuverability

Maneuverability is sufficient for changing positions and supporting allies; however, aggressive frontline gameplay requires caution.

Pros: Stable forward speed. Confident behavior on roads and hard surfaces. Good handling at medium distances. Comfortable turret traverse speed.

Cons: Inferior in dynamics to lighter medium tanks. Noticeable loss of speed on soft ground. Weak reverse speed, making it difficult to retreat quickly after firing.

Armor and Survivability

Pros: The frontal hull has rational slope angles, which occasionally allows for ricochets. With lucky angles, the turret can withstand hits from small-caliber guns. Spaced crew positioning sometimes reduces the effect of single hits.

Cons: Frontal armor is penetrated by most guns of its rank. Sides and rear are extremely vulnerable. The high profile increases the likelihood of taking damage.

Upon Penetration: The ammo rack is often hit, leading to instantaneous destruction. Potential loss of several crew members in one hit, which sharply reduces the vehicle's combat effectiveness.

Armament

75-mm M3 Gun

Pros: Good accuracy and predictable ballistics. High rate of fire — approximately 5 seconds with a fully upgraded crew. Excellent vertical guidance angles, allowing for effective play from the terrain. Effective against light and medium tanks of its rank. A single-plane stabilizer significantly improves firing comfort on the move and during stops.

Cons: Limited armor penetration against well-protected targets. Requires aiming for weak spots on heavy tanks. Damage is inconsistent with imprecise hits.

Features

Comfortable for playing from the terrain and ridges. Well-suited for supporting allies from the second line. Versatile and stable in most combat situations. Forgives minor mistakes for beginners but punishes gross positioning errors.

Conclusions and Comments

The M4A2 is a reliable and balanced early-rank medium tank in War Thunder Mobile, oriented toward calm and thoughtful gameplay. It combines a comfortable gun, decent mobility, and shooting ease, making it suitable for most maps and game situations.

However, the tank is not designed for head-on duels and requires smart positioning, especially when encountering well-armored opponents.

Key Takeaways: • Excellent choice for learning basic positional gameplay. • Comfortable and stable armament. • Effective at medium distances and on rough terrain. • Poorly suited for head-on trades and aggressive lane pushing.
