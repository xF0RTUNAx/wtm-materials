Rank 9 IT-1

Historical Background

The IT-1 is a Soviet missile tank based on the T-62 chassis, developed in the 1960s as a specialized ATGM platform featuring the 3M7 "Drakon" remote-controlled missiles. It was mass-produced in small quantities and is presented as a niche vehicle with an automated ATGM launcher and a low-profile turret.

Quick Overview

Maneuverability

Pros: Engine/Power: V-55 diesel, ~580 hp; combat weight ≈35 t — power-to-weight ratio ≈17 hp/t. Maximum speed: ~55 km/h. The mobility of the T-62 chassis provides sufficient movement for repositioning.

Cons: Maneuverability is inferior to light armored vehicles; high inertia during sharp turns. Off-road capability and acceleration are slower than fast reconnaissance vehicles.

Armor and Survivability

Pros: Features the T-62 chassis with typical frontal hull armor for the era; the low silhouette reduces visibility and the target area. The simple and reliable internal layout makes repairs easier.

Cons: Sides, rear, and roof are vulnerable; weak protection against HE shells and overhead fire. The lack of a large-caliber cannon makes the vehicle entirely dependent on the operational status of the ATGM system.

Armament

Primary Weapon: 3M7 "Drakon" ATGM Launcher

Ammunition:

3M7 "Drakon" (ATGM)

TNT equivalent weight: ~3.15 kg. Armor penetration: ~500 mm.

Secondary Armament:

7.62 mm PKT coaxial machine gun.

Features

A fully specialized ATGM platform: primary armament consists of 3M7 guided missiles with high armor penetration for its rank. The absence of a traditional gun limits effectiveness in close combat and when suppressing light targets. Requires distance control, camouflage, and cover from allies.

Conclusions and Comments

The IT-1 is a niche machine that is highly effective against heavy armored targets at medium and long ranges thanks to the 3M7 "Drakon," but it is vulnerable in close combat or if the launcher components are hit. Use cover, change positions frequently, and rely on ally support; avoid open close-range duels and flank fire.
