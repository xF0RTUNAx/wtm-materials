Rank 13 Leopard 2A4M CAN

Historical Background

In the mid-2000s, the Canadians began looking for a replacement for the Leopard C2A1 Mexas tanks, which needed to have good resistance against anti-tank mines and improvised explosive devices. In 2007, Canada purchased 80 Leopard 2A4 tanks from the Netherlands, and in 2009, commissioned the German company Krauss-Maffei Wegmann to repair and upgrade 20 Leopard 2A4 tanks to the Leopard 2A4M CAN standard; the remaining 60 tanks were used as training vehicles. The Leopard 2A4M CAN tanks were equipped with additional composite armor packages to protect the front and sides of the turret and hull. The rear parts of the turret and hull were reinforced with slat (cage) armor against HEAT projectiles. The fire control system was upgraded, and the tank received a modern thermal imager and a commander's sight. Due to the increased weight of the tank, the brakes and suspension were modified. By 2010, all 20 tanks had been upgraded, and between 2010 and 2011, five Leopard 2A4M CAN tanks were deployed in Afghanistan.

Brief Overview

Maneuverability

Pros: Good maneuverability metrics in all aspects, as befits NATO tanks. A benchmark for horizontal and vertical targeting speeds.

Cons: There may be less responsiveness in handling during sharp maneuvers.

Armor and Survivability

In practice - a lack of reliable armor. Accidental or lucky bounces/ricochets are possible. The greatest risks arise when taking damage to the sides. The frontal turret armor can perform better, especially in combination with cyclical maneuvers, but there is still a risk of taking systematic damage.

Additionally: Do not take more than 16 shells for better survivability. You can angle the turret to the right to try and increase survivability.

Armament

The penetration of the top APFSDS (darts) is sufficient to confidently penetrate all opponents. Good and fairly consistent damage.

Keep in mind that in terms of reload speed, we only outpace the Soviet autoloader. It is also demanding on crew upgrades to achieve the best reload time.

Features

Blowout panels. Dual fire control system. Inability to shoot backward due to a design feature.

Conclusions and Comments

Recommended primarily for experienced players. A technical tank whose potential is unlocked considering knowledge of the theoretical base, the presence of gaming experience, and the ability to utilize its specific advantages while neutralizing permanent disadvantages.
