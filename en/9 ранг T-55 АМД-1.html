Rank 9 T-55 AMD-1

Historical Background

The T-54/T-55 is a series of Soviet medium tanks developed in the late 1940s and early 1950s at Plant No. 183 (Nizhny Tagil). The first prototype, T-54, was ready by the end of 1945; mass production and export of the T-55 began in 1958. The series was characterized by its simplicity, extensive standardization, and massive scale: a total of approximately 96,000–100,000 units were produced. The T-55 underwent numerous modernizations and export variations; the AMD-1 modification is one of the later upgrades, featuring active protection and improved electronics/armament intended to extend the combat effectiveness of the base design.

Brief Overview

Maneuverability

Pros: Engine/Power: A modernized diesel (~520–620 hp depending on upgrades) at a weight of ~36–38 t provides a power-to-weight ratio of 13–17 hp/t, ensuring steady acceleration on the battlefield. Top Speed: ~50–55 km/h on roads depending on the engine and transmission state. Good mobility in moderate off-road conditions and quick repositioning at close ranges.

Cons: Handling and dynamics are inferior to more modern main battle tanks; noticeable inertia and body roll during sharp maneuvers. Cross-country capability on very soft ground is limited due to track width and weight.

Armor and Survivability

Pros: Improved frontal protection: Upper hull front and turret with original hardened armor; in-game, the front is capable of withstanding many standard rounds of its rank when hit in the central zone. The AMD-1 is equipped with the "Drozd" active protection system (80° sector)—it reflects/intercepts certain ATGMs and rockets in the forward arc. Simple and reliable layout provides good repairability and survivability under damage.

Cons: Sides and rear are vulnerable: Side projections are easily penetrated by most modern shells of its rank. Top armor and the turret roof are weak points against unguided rockets/HE shells and overhead fire.

Armament

Main Gun: 100mm D-10T2S rifled gun with 1–2 plane stabilization. Highly effective at medium ranges with a good rate of fire (~6–8 rounds/min under combat conditions).

Shells:

3BM25 (APFSDS)

Armor penetration: 330mm at 100 meters; 310mm at 500 meters.

9M117 (ATGM - barrel launched)

TNT equivalent: 4.7kg. Armor penetration: 600mm at 100 meters; 600mm at 500 meters. Fuse sensitivity: 0.1mm.

BR-412D (APCBC)

TNT equivalent: 93g. Armor penetration: 240mm at 100 meters; 220mm at 500 meters. Fuse sensitivity: 19mm.

OF-412 (HE)

TNT equivalent: 1.46kg. Armor penetration: 19mm at 100 meters; 19mm at 500 meters. Fuse sensitivity: 0.1mm.

3BK17M (HEAT-FS)

TNT equivalent: 1.17kg. Armor penetration: 390mm at 100 meters; 390mm at 500 meters. Fuse sensitivity: 0.1mm.

Secondary Armament:

1 x 12.7mm DShK (roof) — ~600–850 rounds/min, effective against aircraft and light vehicles. 1 x 7.62mm PKT (coaxial).

Features

"Drozd" APS — Forward 80° protection, can intercept a limited set of ATGMs and rockets. Presence of smoke grenades/launchers.

Conclusions and Comments

The T-55 AMD-1 is a hybrid of classic and modern: the classic mobile T-55 platform with improved frontal protection and the Drozd APS. It is strong in tactical close-to-mid range encounters: use APFSDS and the guided 9M117 to defeat heavy targets at medium/long ranges. The main weaknesses are the sides/rear and the turret roof; avoid overhead fire and flank attacks. Active protection provides an advantage against ATGMs, but it isn't absolute—be mindful of its reload and operational status.
