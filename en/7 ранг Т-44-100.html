7 rank T-44-100

Historical Background

The T-44-100 is a modification of the Soviet T-44 medium tank. It differs from the base version by being equipped with a 100 mm D-10T gun, a 12.7 mm DShK anti-aircraft machine gun, turret side armor thickened by 10 mm, and the presence of 6 mm side screens.

The development of this T-44 version was carried out in 1943-1944 due to the appearance of newer heavy tanks in Germany, which surpassed the base version in range and firepower. The T-44-100 prototype appeared in 1945, but it did not completely satisfy the military, and later the project was completely canceled due to the start of mass production of the more modern T-54.

Brief Overview

Maneuverability

Pros:

  • Excellent dynamics: Fast acceleration and a high top speed allow you to take key positions.
  • High maneuverability: The tank handles perfectly, confidently "circling" heavy tanks and tank destroyers.
  • Good cross-country ability: High ground resistance characteristics (about 0.4) provide excellent speed even on soft surfaces.
  • Constant movement: Maneuverability allows the use of "shoot-and-hide" tactics, surviving through speed.

Cons:

  • Armor: Despite the speed, the tank is lightly armored, so a maneuvering mistake leads to quick annihilation.
  • Handling specifics: Although the dynamics are excellent, some players note that the acceleration could be slightly faster, though this is more of a nitpick.

Armor and Survivability

  • Hull front (UFP/LFP): 90 mm at steep angles (62°), which gives high effective armor, however, the LFP is vulnerable.
  • Turret front: Reinforced to 100-120 mm.
  • Sides: 75 mm (hull) + 6 mm side screens.
  • Vulnerabilities: Dense layout — the driver and ammo rack are often hit when penetrated in the front or side.

Armament

Main armament:

  • Gun: 100 mm LB-1 (or D-10T).
  • Ammunition: Armor-piercing shells with a penetration of about 190-205 mm and APCR/APFSDS (up to 247-258 mm).
  • Rate of fire: About 8 rounds per minute.
  • Features: Presence of a muzzle brake on the LB-1.

Additional armament:

  • Machine guns: Coaxial 7.62 mm DT (1890 rounds) and anti-aircraft 12.7 mm DShK (250 rounds).

Conclusions and Comments

In battle, the T-44-100 requires proper use of terrain to hide the vulnerable lower glacis plate (LFP). The 6 mm side screens help perfectly when side-scraping, saving you from HEAT shells.

Use the superb maneuverability to flank clumsy opponents and always keep moving, as the dense layout inside the tank does not forgive direct hits.
