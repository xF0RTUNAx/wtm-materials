2. Destroying Bases and Airfields

Information is based on practical experience. There may be errors or inaccuracies.
  • Base Destruction:
    At ranks 3 and 9
    Based on experience, 4 MK84 2000-lb bombs from the rank 8 A-1H were enough to destroy a base. Thus, to destroy a base in one drop, you need at least 2315 kg of TNT equivalent (originally Composition H6).

    At ranks 4-6

    Previously, it required at least 1524 kg of TNT equivalent (originally Torpex). Now this value should be lower.

    Other ranks

    3 Mk 13 Mod 0 mines from the rank 8 F7F-3 were enough to destroy a base. Thus, to destroy a base in one drop, you need at least 1524 kg of TNT equivalent (originally Torpex).
  • Airfield Destruction:
    At ranks 3 and 9
    Based on experience, 8 SB2500 bombs from the rank 7 Ju 288 C were enough to destroy an airfield. Thus, to destroy an airfield, you need at least 21888 kg of TNT equivalent (originally Trialen 105).

    At ranks 4-6

    Previously, it required at least 15099 kg of TNT equivalent (originally Tritonal). Now this value should be lower.

    Other ranks

    4 AN-M56 4000-lb bombs from the rank 8 B-29A-BN were enough to destroy an airfield. Thus, to completely destroy an airfield, you need at least 15099 kg of TNT equivalent (originally Tritonal).

3. About Certain Ground Attack Weapons (Suspended Armament)

1. HVAR Rocket
Great for attacking SPAA and artillery. Not suitable for attacking pillboxes and tanks. A slight deviation from the target is acceptable while maintaining attack success due to sufficient TNT equivalent and warhead type. Ideally: 1 rocket – 1 target.

2. Zuni Mk32 Rocket
Suitable for attacking SPAA, artillery, tanks, and pillboxes. Deviation from the target is not recommended due to the warhead type, but the large TNT equivalent can save the situation. It's important to be prepared to use multiple rockets, especially against tanks and pillboxes, due to the difficulty of aiming during dynamic combat.

3. FFAR Mighty Mouse Rocket
Suitable for attacking SPAA, artillery, tanks, and pillboxes. Deviation from the target is not acceptable due to the warhead type and small TNT equivalent. It's important to be prepared to use a large number of rockets, especially against tanks and pillboxes, due to the difficulty of aiming during dynamic combat.

4. M134 Minigun
Suitable for attacking SPAA, artillery, and tanks. Not suitable for destroying pillboxes. Deviation from the target is not acceptable. Works excellent against enemy aviation as well. It is very important to get used to the ballistics, and attacks at minimum distances (less than approximately 0.3-0.4 km) are recommended.

5. Tiny Tim
Suitable for attacking SPAA, artillery, tanks, and pillboxes. Deviation from heavy targets (pillboxes, tanks) is not recommended. Launched similarly to unguided rockets. Requires more lead due to the launch mechanics. It's important not to launch too close and low to the target, as the splash damage can damage your own aircraft.

6. RBS-132
Suitable for attacking SPAA, artillery, tanks, and pillboxes. Deviation from the target is not recommended due to the warhead type, but the large TNT equivalent can save the situation. It's important to be prepared to use multiple rockets, especially against tanks and pillboxes, due to the difficulty of aiming during dynamic combat.

4. Engaging Ground Targets

Note that the type of explosive affects the effectiveness of engaging certain targets!

To destroy a pillbox, 1 x 500-lb AN-M64A1 bomb (163 kg TNT, originally Composition B) is enough; or 4 x 250-lb AN-M57 bombs (58.5 kg TNT).

Conditional Classification:

  1. Small (up to 1 kg TNT): destroyed by a direct hit on open-topped vehicles, SPAA, and artillery.
  2. Medium (1-5 kg TNT): destroyed on open-topped vehicles, SPAA, and artillery, even with a slight deviation from the target. Heavier targets require a direct hit, specifically in vulnerable spots, using a large number of rockets (i.e., not very effective).
  3. Heavy (5-10 kg TNT): freely destroys open-topped vehicles, SPAA, and artillery, even with increased deviation from the target. Still requires precise aiming at vulnerable spots on heavier targets, including the use of a large number of rockets.
  4. Superheavy (> 10 kg TNT): their impact can be considered similar to bombs, but with rocket mechanics.

HEAT: all require high precision hitting weak spots. It is important to expect to use a large number of rockets, especially against heavy targets. Unlike HE, using them against heavy targets is effective, albeit with some peculiarities compared to bombs.

6. Fighter Roles

Ammunition belt recommendations:

  • Caliber under 20mm: Maximum AP-I/API-T
  • Caliber over 20mm: Maximum FI-T/HEF-I

  • Against fighters: you can prioritize fragmentation-high-explosive (HEF) and high-explosive (HE) shells, as these are effective against lightly armored or unarmored targets. Shrapnel easily destroys aircraft skin, and HE shells can literally snap the wings off enemy fighters. Choose belts with the highest number of HEF, HE, incendiary, etc., shells.

  • Against bombers: the belt should include both HEF and armor-piercing (AP) shells. AP shells break the aircraft's structural framework, penetrate armor plates, etc., which is highly relevant for taking down bombers. A good belt option for bombers would be a mix of two HEF and one AP shell. As for machine guns, belts with a maximum amount of API and AP bullets are recommended, as they literally allow you to "saw off" enemy wings or set fuel tanks on fire.

7. Effective Aircraft

Aircraft Comments
IX BI Unique and partly ultimate flight characteristics (FM) with difficult implementation and limited armament. Premium, recommended mostly for experienced pilots.
IX Me 163B Unique and ultimate aircraft for most types of battles with challenging but extremely powerful armament. Recommended for all players.
IX F-80C-10 Decent flight characteristics, with a decent set of suspended armament and weak forward-firing armament. Recommended as a high-tier attacker.
VIII Tu-2S-59 Best attacker for the USSR. Has defensive armament covering multiple angles, allowing reaction to enemies. Recommended for both high and low altitude ground attack.
VIII A-1H Best attacker in the game. The massive payload allows for versatile playstyles. It's crucial to avoid dogfights. Became harder to play after the introduction of jets.
VIII AD-4 Budget version of the A-1H. Essentially the same excellent attacker, but with fewer capabilities. Also going through tough times after the introduction of jets.
VII A7M1 (NK9H) Ultimate fighter for close and maneuverable dogfights. Has some combat limitations. Generally recommended for everyone during sales.
