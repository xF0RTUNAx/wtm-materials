T-64BV

Historical Background

The T-64BV is a variant of the T-64 medium tank with the "Kontakt-1" explosive reactive armor (ERA) system and an improved 125-mm 2A46M-1 gun. Equipped with a 6ETs40 autoloader, it combines compact dimensions and increased ammunition penetration with the presence of ERA on the hull and turret.

Brief Overview

Maneuverability

Pros:

  • Good mobility — maximum highway speed reaches 66 km/h.
  • Decent power-to-weight ratio of ≈16–17 hp/t.
  • Compact dimensions make it easier to maneuver and use the terrain.

Cons:

  • Limited reverse speed.
  • Sensitive to soft ground.
  • Sluggishness during sharp maneuvers due to its mass of about 40 tons.

Armor and Survivability

Pros:

  • The presence of "Kontakt-1" ERA on the frontal part of the hull and turret reduces the effectiveness of HEAT ammunition.
  • The turret and gun mantlet can withstand many hits at its rank.

Cons:

  • The lower and upper glacis plates (LFP, UFP), as well as the upper projections of the tank, are vulnerable.
  • The sides and rear are penetrated by most APFSDS shells of its own and higher ranks.
  • The ammunition is partially located in vulnerable zones, which increases the risk of detonation upon penetration.

Armament and Features

Main gun: 125-mm 2A46M-1 gun with 6ETs40 autoloader.

Ammunition: ≈36 rounds (some in the autoloader, some in reserve); presence of 9M112 ATGMs.

Shells:

  • APFSDS (analog to 3BM42/3BM42M) — high initial armor penetration, effective at range.
  • HEAT (analog to 3BK18M) — stable penetration against weak spots.
  • HE (High-Explosive) — excellent choice against light vehicles.

Additionally: coaxial machine guns, smoke grenades.

Features: the autoloader provides a high rate of fire regardless of the crew's condition; explosive reactive armor (ERA) blocks significantly reduce the risk of HEAT damage; the tank has relatively good vertical guidance (gun depression/elevation).

Conclusions and Comments

The T-64BV is a maneuverable medium tank with a powerful 125-mm gun and effective explosive reactive armor. The main gameplay tactic: use mobility and terrain, take covered positions, and accurately hit the enemy's weak spots.

It is extremely important to avoid prolonged frontal duels with top APFSDS shells and unexpected attacks from open flanks, as the side armor and ammunition layout do not forgive positioning mistakes.
