Rank 6 Tiger II

Historical Background

The Panzerkampfwagen Tiger II ("King Tiger") was developed as an evolution of the Tiger I. The German command sought a heavy tank with reinforced frontal armor and a more powerful gun capable of countering new Soviet vehicles featuring sloped armor. By late 1943, the final design was approved with a 150mm frontal plate, sloped armor, and a new Henschel turret. Serial production continued until March 1945, with approximately 489 units produced. In War Thunder Mobile, the Tiger II retains its key characteristics: a sturdy front, a powerful cannon, and vulnerable sides, requiring careful and deliberate positioning.

Quick Overview

Maneuverability

Pros: Decent straight-line acceleration for a heavy tank. Sufficient speed to occupy key positions at the start of a match. Stationary turns are relatively comfortable—maneuvering the hull is easier than with most other heavies.

Cons: Heavy hull—noticeably loses speed on soft ground, inclines, and during turns. Retreating from flank attacks is difficult. Mobility drops practically to zero if the engine is damaged. Large dimensions make hiding the hull difficult.

Additionally: Mobility is sufficient for reliable positional play but is not suited for aggressive pressure or flanking maneuvers.

Armor and Survivability

The hull front is one of the best at its level, especially with slight "diamond" angling. The turret also possesses good protection if you don't expose the gun mantlet at flat angles. It frequently deflects APHE and weak sub-caliber rounds.

The sides are seriously vulnerable and can be penetrated by almost any tank. The large silhouette makes it an easy target for artillery and airstrikes. Penetrations through the gun mantlet or the lower front plate (LFP) often lead to critical damage: breech, driver, or ammo rack. The double side layer does not save you—hits at the correct angle result in penetration with detonation.

Warning: HEAT rounds, flank shots, and top-down attacks are particularly dangerous.

Armament

Main Gun: 88 mm KwK 43 L/71.

Excellent penetration for a heavy tank of its rank. High damage—often destroys opponents in 1–2 hits. Superior accuracy and stability for medium and long-range firing. Easily knocks out key modules: engine, transmission, breech, and crew members.

The reload isn't the fastest—misses are critical. Against heavy Soviet machinery, precise aiming at weak zones is required. In close combat, it loses out to more maneuverable vehicles.

Practical Effect in WTM: The Tiger II's gun guarantees consistent damage and duel superiority with proper positioning and getting the first shot off.

Features

Best results are achieved with correct hull angling. The gameplay shines at medium distances—where the combination of armor and firepower is most effective. Requires constant monitoring of the flanks. It performs confidently in urban environments if you manage to cover your sides. Good compatibility with team play: support and lane control increase its influence on the battle.

Conclusions and Comments

The Tiger II is for players who prefer: positional play, reliable frontal tanking, accurate shots at medium ranges, and calm, controlled combat.

With correct positioning and smart angling, the Tiger II is capable of dominating most peers. However, attempts to play aggressively or exposing the sides lead to quick destruction—its weak zones are easily exploited even by lighter tanks.
