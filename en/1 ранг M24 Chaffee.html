Rank 1 M24 Chaffee

Historical Background

The M24 Chaffee light tank was developed in the USA near the end of World War II as a replacement for the outdated M5A1 Stuart. The main goal for designers was to create a light vehicle with a full-fledged 75mm gun, comparable in firepower to medium tanks. Production began in 1944, and the M24 quickly established itself as a better-armed and more comfortable light tank. It saw combat in the final stages of the war, was actively used in Korea, and was supplied to US allies. In War Thunder Mobile, the M24 retains its historical concept: a fast and maneuverable scout with minimal armor, but good armament for its rank. The M24 is a light tank for dynamic gameplay. It combines excellent mobility, a comfortable gun, and wide opportunities for flanking attacks. However, the vehicle has practically no armor — any mistake in positioning can lead to instant destruction.

Brief Overview

Maneuverability

One of the best maneuverabilities at its rank — the vehicle is built for speed, flanking, and aggressive play.

Pros: High top speed in a straight line. Fast acceleration — allows you to take key positions before enemies do. Good traverse speed and responsive controls. Excellent mobility on soft terrain — speed loss is insignificant. Acceptable reverse speed, allowing for a timely retreat after firing.

Cons: Handling at high speeds requires precision. Light mass — the tank quickly loses momentum during collisions and after taking damage.

Armor and Survivability

Pros: Low mass and relatively compact silhouette can help avoid hits in some situations. Bounces are possible at extreme angles thanks to the turret shape.

Cons: Practically no armor — can be penetrated by all tanks at its rank and above. The crew and modules are densely packed, which amplifies the damage effect upon penetration. The large profile of the turret is easily hit even at medium distances.

When penetrated: Fatal hits to the ammo rack are common, leading to instant destruction. The enemy often takes out most of the crew, rendering the tank completely combat-ineffective.

Armament

Main gun: 75 mm M6

Pros: Pleasant ballistics — easy to work with at medium distances. Good rate of fire for its rank. Sufficient armor penetration for shooting at sides and weak spots. Fast targeting — convenient for firing on the move. The presence of a single-plane stabilizer improves firing comfort while moving.

Cons: Penetration is often insufficient against the front of heavy and well-armored vehicles. Damage is inconsistent — precise aiming is required. Effectiveness drops significantly at long distances.

Practical effect in WTM: The gun is perfectly suited for flanking and finishing off targets. The M24 is especially dangerous if it shoots first and remains unnoticed.

Features

Ideal for scouting, flanking maneuvers, and fast breakthroughs. Excellent for capturing and intercepting points. Requires constant movement — a stationary target doesn't live long. It is critically important to play based on speed and the element of surprise.

Conclusions and Comments

The M24 in War Thunder Mobile is a tank for players who prefer dynamics, mobility, and an aggressive style. It fully reveals its potential if you: flank enemies, use the terrain, act unexpectedly, shoot first. At the same time, the vehicle is absolutely unsuitable for frontal shootouts — there is no armor, and any mistake becomes fatal.
