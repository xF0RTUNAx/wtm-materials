Armor Resistance Coefficients
Tank Tracks
Equivalent to rolled armor (1 to 1).
20mm tracks = 20mm armor
Aluminum USA (Alloy 5083)
3 times weaker than rolled 30mm aluminum.
For example: a shell with a fuse sensitivity of 19mm will not detonate on a 30mm layer of aluminum.
30mm Al 5038 = 10mm rolled Al
Aluminum USSR
Alloy ABT-101/102. Resistance coefficient ~0.4 (stronger than American).
30mm Al ABT-101/102 = 12mm rolled Al
Aluminum (France / China)
French (Alloy 7039) and Chinese (unmarked). The strongest types of aluminum in the game.
16mm Al = 8mm rolled armor
High Hardness Rolled Armor
Provides 30% better penetration resistance than standard rolled armor.
10mm High Hardness = 13mm standard
Cast Homogeneous Armor
5% weaker than rolled homogeneous armor.
100mm cast = 95mm rolled
Structural Steel
5 times weaker than rolled armor!
10mm steel = 2mm rolled armor
Chassis Structural Steel
2 times weaker than rolled armor.
20mm chassis = 10mm rolled armor
Wood
20% weaker than rolled homogeneous armor. Often found on older vehicles.
15mm wood = 12mm homogeneous armor
Anti-Radiation Material
Extremely weak protection, 80% weaker than rolled armor.
10mm material = 2mm rolled armor
Bulletproof Glass
Characteristics depend on the country of origin!
Britain: 60mm = 12mm (80% weaker)
Italy: 30mm = 8mm
Gun Steel
Extreme durability. Exact value unknown, but at least 7 times stronger than rolled homogeneous armor.
20mm gun steel ≈ 150mm rolled homogeneous armor
Textolite
Against HEAT: 30% weaker (100mm = 70mm).
Against APFSDS: 60% weaker (100mm = 40mm).
NERA
Against APFSDS: 6 times weaker than rolled homogeneous (300mm = 50mm).
Against HEAT: More effective (300mm = 140mm).
Metal-Polymer
Almost useless against darts (120mm = 8mm), but holds up well against HEAT (120mm = 100mm).
Non-obvious Lifehacks and Loopholes
Shooting Through Textures

Fences (on the American Desert map, but generally, this fence type applies to any map): Do not stop HEAT, HE shells, or ATGMs. You can safely and effectively fire through them.

Also, this type of fence can be penetrated by ATGMs on any map where they exist:

Containers: The first dart shot passes right through them on the Seaport map.

Effective Bombing in Tank Campaign: When dropping bombs from an altitude of approximately 800-850 m, the bombs will detonate immediately upon impact, leaving the enemy no chance to react. By the way, if you hear the characteristic sound of a falling bomb, you are most likely safe.

ATGM Interception

An ATGM can be shot down with a machine gun, a main gun shell, or a counter-ATGM.

Defense Against Top-Attack Munitions:

Top-attack munitions explode by reacting to an ATGM directly beneath them. Or additionally, by raising your gun barrel which the enemy ATGM will detonate against.

If such a missile is flying at you — launch your ATGM below the enemy's trajectory. The enemy missile will "see" your missile beneath it and detonate in mid-air, while your projectile will continue towards the enemy.

Important! You can only use an ATGM to shoot down a top-attack missile by flying underneath it. Bullets and regular shells are too fast and small to trigger the sensor.
Spoofing Radio Fuses (SAMs)

Surface-to-Air Missiles (SAMs) often feature a radio fuse. If one is flying head-on at you, fire a barrage of unguided rockets (UGRs) toward it. As they pass nearby, they will force the missile to detonate prematurely.

Works perfectly on the A-10 Warthog thanks to its large reserve of Hydra rockets.
