Rank 9 Leopard A1A1

Historical Background

The Leopard A1A1 is a modification of the German Leopard I main battle tank, designed to improve the combat performance of the first production series. While the Leopard I was successful and met initial requirements, it had shortcomings, particularly its weak armor. Feedback from early service led to the 1970 modernization program to the Leopard A1 standard: these tanks received passive night vision, a new track design, side skirts, and most importantly, a two-plane stabilizer for the main gun. Between 1975 and 1977, all Leopard A1s were further upgraded to the A1A1 variant, featuring Blohm & Voss spaced turret armor to increase resistance against HEAT munitions. From 1979, the Bundeswehr began replacing Leopard 1s with the Leopard 2, with the last units retired in 2003. Despite this, over 15 countries continue to operate various modifications of the Leopard today.

Brief Overview

Mobility

Pros: Excellent mobility — the tank's primary strength. A powerful 830 hp engine, high power-to-weight ratio (~20 hp/t), and good transmission. Fast reverse speed (28 km/h), high top speed (~72 km/h), and excellent maneuverability on rough terrain.

Cons: The rear placement of the transmission makes the tank more vulnerable from the back. At high speeds, handling can feel somewhat twitchy.

Armor and Survivability

The low silhouette and good hull armor slope angles can result in unexpected ricochets.

Armor is essentially symbolic. The hull (70 mm at its thickest point) and turret can be penetrated by anything, including heavy machine guns (from the side and rear). Typical three-man hull layout: a precise frontal hit has a high chance of knocking them all out. High explosive risk.

Armament

Primary: 105mm Royal Ordnance L7A3 rifled gun — a NATO legend.

Ammunition:

DM23 (APFSDS): Excellent armor penetration (~340 mm), allows for reliably engaging any target at its rank.

DM12 (HEAT-FS): Effective against lightly armored targets, open-top SPAAGs, etc.

DM512 (HESH): Effective against medium-armored targets, explodes with great force.

DM13 (APDS): Effective against thick armor, dealing damage via secondary spalling.

Additionally: 2 x 7.62mm machine guns.

Features

Smoke Grenades: 8 launchers, very useful for retreating or deploying smoke screens. Vertical plane stabilizer.

Conclusions and Comments

The Leopard A1A1 is a classic "glass cannon." It is a tank for the "first shot — first hit — first out" tactic. Its main assets are its speed and low profile. This vehicle is intended for patient, calculated gameplay, holding key positions, and supporting allies from a distance.
