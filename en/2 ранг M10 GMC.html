Rank 2 M10 GMC

Historical Background

The M10 GMC is an American self-propelled anti-tank gun built on the chassis of the M4 Sherman medium tank. The main feature of the vehicle was its fully rotating turret equipped with a 3-inch (76.2 mm) M7 anti-tank gun, which provided high efficiency against armored targets.

The M10 was actively used by the Allies during World War II in both Europe and the Pacific Theater, where it proved itself with high firepower, good rate of fire, and versatility.

In War Thunder Mobile, the M10 GMC retains its historical role as a mobile tank destroyer with an emphasis on medium and long-range combat, where its armament and mobility are most effective.

Brief Overview

Maneuverability

Maneuverability allows for timely position changes and fire correction but is not designed for sudden raids or aggressive breakthroughs.

Pros: Stable forward movement speed. Good stability on roads and comfortable handling. Relatively easy hull turning thanks to sufficient engine power. Convenient movement between firing positions.

Cons: Average dynamics on inclines — inferior to light tanks. Noticeable loss of speed and handling on soft ground. Weak reverse speed — makes it difficult to quickly back into cover after a shot.

Armor and Survivability

Pros: Hull slopes reduce the likelihood of direct penetration at medium distances. Side armor occasionally saves from small-caliber gun fire. The internal hull volume increases the crew's chances of survival during partial penetrations.

Cons: Thin armor — most anti-tank guns can penetrate the vehicle from any distance. The turret is particularly vulnerable: hits often disable the gun or the crew. The high silhouette makes the M10 an easy target in open areas.

Upon Penetration: Ammunition or gun mechanisms are frequently damaged. Loss of crew members sharply reduces the vehicle's combat effectiveness.

Armament

3″ (76.2 mm) Anti-tank Gun M7

Pros: High armor penetration — confidently engages most medium and heavy tanks of its rank. Excellent rate of fire — provides high fire density in duel situations. Stable ballistics and good accuracy, convenient for stationary firing. Good vertical guidance angles — convenient for using terrain and cover.

Cons: The heavy gun requires strict position control. Effective long-range shooting requires the ability to predict target movement. The vehicle's weak armor limits the ability to play confidently in open spaces. Lack of stabilization negatively affects the convenience of firing on the move.

Features

The M7 gun makes the M10 a powerful anti-tank machine at medium and long ranges. Thin armor forces constant play from cover and terrain. The high silhouette requires particularly careful positioning. Perfect for positional support of allies and a "sniper" playstyle.

Conclusions and Comments

The M10 GMC is a powerful and accurate anti-tank vehicle oriented toward positional gameplay in War Thunder Mobile.

Key Conclusions: Suitable for playing from cover and at safe distances. Effective against most medium and heavy tanks of its rank. Extremely weak armor makes direct close-range encounters very dangerous. Requires careful positioning and smart use of terrain. A great choice for players who prefer a "sniper" style and supporting allies from a distance.
