Rank 10 2S25M

Historical Background

The 2S25M "Sprut-SDM1" is a modification of the Russian airborne self-propelled anti-tank gun 2S25 "Sprut-SD". The prototype of the 2S25M "Sprut-SDM1" was first publicly demonstrated at the International Military-Technical Forum "Army-2015". During modernization, the fire control system was improved, and a machine gun was added to repel air attacks.

Brief Overview

Maneuverability

Pros: Good maneuverability across all aspects, allowing it to quickly reach necessary positions and react in time to battlefield challenges.

Cons: Vehicle control becomes difficult, especially at high speeds, due to its large dimensions.

Armor and Survivability

The limit of its protection is small-caliber machine guns. Everything else poses a significant threat. Machine guns and APFSDS (darts) may require more time and hits to destroy the Sprut due to its large fighting compartment. However, HEAT and HE ammunition are lethal to us.

Armament

Main gun: 125-mm 2A75-1. Can fire both standard shells and ATGMs.

It features an autoloader, providing a constant reload time regardless of the crew. It's worth noting that its reload speed falls behind most NATO vehicles, especially at higher ranks.

More or less optimal overall values for turret traverse and gun elevation speed allow it to keep up with enemy actions.

A notable drawback is the poor gun depression angles, which must always be kept in mind.

The top APFSDS (3BM60) and ATGM are the primary shells for regular gameplay. The dart has enough penetration and damage. Practice has shown that the ATGM is an even more versatile round. The main thing is not to forget about ATGM features (e.g., clearing the area before launch and technical countermeasures), but they will reward you with good penetration and, crucially, excellent damage.

Additionally: the machine gun is only useful for clearing obstacles.

Features

Dual fire control system. The vehicle is amphibious.

Conclusions and Comments

The vehicle can truly shine in the hands of experienced players who prefer a mostly deliberate gameplay style, but know exactly when to play more aggressively. It performs excellently in a support role, for ambushes, and flanking attacks. It plays well not only at its native rank but also at the very top tiers. In most cases, it's better to spawn it in the second half of the battle when the situation is more stable. Of course, it's important not to forget about the tank's fragility and other weaknesses. Because of this, beginners might find it harder to have an effective and enjoyable game, but the Sprut clearly has potential.
