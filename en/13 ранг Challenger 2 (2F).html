Rank 13 Challenger 2 (2F)

Historical Background

The Challenger 2 (Dorchester Level 2F) is a modification of the British Challenger 2 main battle tank. The 2003 Iraq War, in which the Challenger 2 took a direct part, revealed the tank's vulnerability to fire from handheld anti-tank grenade launchers. In 2004, Challenger 2 tanks first began to be equipped with additional Dorchester armor packages to protect against RPG fire. This armor package provides an additional level of protection compared to the previous Dorchester 2E and includes additional "Chobham" armor, "ROMOR-A" explosive reactive armor on the frontal plates, as well as Dorchester Level 2F blocks along the sides of the hull. Furthermore, the sides of the turret also received external "Chobham" armor packages, increasing the protection of the crew inside the fighting compartment from potential flank attacks.

Brief Overview

Maneuverability

Pros: The reverse speed allows for a successful retreat from threats. The Brit also possesses good responsiveness.

Cons: The Challenger's maneuverability potential is inferior to all modern tanks. Nevertheless, due to small maps and the arcade nature of the battles, this is not as noticeable and is sufficient for regular situations. The large dimensions slightly complicate the gameplay.

Armor and Survivability

Snap fire into its silhouette is unlikely to cause noticeable damage, especially while moving. The Brit has standard weak spots inherent to most modern tanks (for example, the lower glacis plate, the gun mantlet, the area under the turret). It is important to remember that the armor only works in the frontal projection, and unnecessary hull angling can lead to tangible damage.

Playing hull-down from the turret, hiding the hull behind cover, is the priority. By combining this playstyle with cyclical maneuvers, outplaying a Challenger becomes a difficult task.

Explosive reactive armor blocks sometimes temporarily save you from HEAT and small-caliber kinetic types of ammunition.

The main thing is not to forget that the sides still take damage very well.

The overall survivability of the tank is quite pleasing. A good, damaging shot can take it out at once, but often 2 or more hits will be required. In this context, the large fighting compartment plays to its advantage by spacing out the key modules.

Armament

The Brit is armed with a high-penetration top APFSDS shell with sufficient post-penetration damage, leaving little to be desired.

The turret traverse and targeting speeds have sufficient values for the game. All of this is complemented by effective vertical guidance angles standard for NATO tanks.

A specific feature lies in the reload speed mechanics. The reload time value stated in the stat card applies to the first-stage ammo rack. After it is depleted, the reload time will increase to approximately 7 seconds.

Losing crew members noticeably increases the reload time.

Machine guns are only useful for clearing obstacles.

Features

A crew member can take over the role of a knocked-out one without wasting time switching seats: the commander can shoot.

Aiming at an enemy directly behind the tank is impossible due to the vehicle's engineering design.

Conclusions and Comments

The primary and best option for utilizing this tank will likely be positional support for allies, hiding the majority of vulnerable zones, with appropriate elements of active and aggressive play.

Despite being largely a copy, the fact of the tank's effectiveness and practicality is not negated. For new players, the Challenger is a good combination of Western technology with a slow and survivable gameplay style to delve into the realities of high-rank battles.
