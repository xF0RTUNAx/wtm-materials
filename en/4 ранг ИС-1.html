Rank 4 IS-1

Historical Background

The IS-1 (Iosif Stalin — IS-1) was an early modification of the Soviet heavy tank family, adopted in 1943 as an intermediate variant between the KV series and the later IS-2. It featured an 85mm D-5T gun for better fire rate and standardization, improved suspension, and reinforced frontal armor compared to its predecessors. It served as the foundation for further series development and the tactical use of heavy vehicles in offensive operations.

Quick Overview

Maneuverability

Pros: Possesses decent mobility for a heavy tank—comparatively better than its heavy predecessors (the KV series). Moderate top speed and good cross-country performance; stable running gear.

Cons: Power-to-weight ratio is lower than that of medium tanks—acceleration and nimbleness are limited. High inertia during sharp maneuvers makes it difficult to change course quickly or engage in high-speed duels.

Armor and Survivability

Pros: Good frontal armor for the early-to-mid war era—effectively protects against most peers at its rank from the front. The hull and turret design provide solid survivability when positioned correctly.

Cons: Side and rear armor are relatively thin—vulnerable to sub-caliber and HEAT rounds. Weak spots include the gun mantlet, armor plate joints, and the commander's cupola. Limited protection for the roof and sides against attacks from above or the flanks.

Armament

Main: 85mm D-5T rifled gun—a good balance of fire rate and armor penetration for its rank. Sighting systems are typical for the period, allowing for effective aimed fire at medium distances.

Shells:

APHE: 140mm penetration; 73g TNT equivalent.

APCR: 190mm penetration.

Cons: Armor penetration is inferior to later 122mm guns and some high-rank 75-88mm guns; requires precise aiming at weak zones against heavily armored foes. Somewhat vulnerable in close-quarters dogfights due to its fire rate compared to light tanks.

Conclusions and Comments

The IS-1 in War Thunder Mobile is a reliable early-rank heavy tank, excellent for positional play and supporting breakthroughs. Strong frontal armor and a balanced gun make it effective against peers when using terrain wisely and aiming precisely at weak spots. However, it is vulnerable to flank attacks and more powerful guns—requiring careful team coordination and positional awareness.
