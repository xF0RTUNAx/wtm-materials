Rank 7 AMX 50 Foch

Historical Background

The AMX 50 Foch is a French experimental self-propelled anti-tank gun developed in the late 1940s on the chassis of the AMX-50 heavy tank as part of France's post-war rearmament program. The vehicle featured a turretless design with heavy frontal armor and a large-caliber gun mounted in the hull (120 and 155 mm variants were designed), intended for engaging heavy tanks at long ranges. It was named in honor of Marshal Ferdinand Foch. Despite its high firepower, good mobility for its class, and rational armor angles, the project never entered serial production due to its complexity, high cost, and the military's shift in interest toward universal tanks with turrets. Therefore, the AMX 50 Foch remained a prototype but later received a second life as a powerful, mobile, yet tactically demanding tank destroyer in gaming interpretations.

Brief Overview

Maneuverability

Pros: High hull traverse speed. Steering and acceleration are very good for its weight class.

Cons: As a casemate vehicle, it lacks a turret, requiring the entire hull to turn to aim at new targets, which can be fatal if the tracks are damaged.

Armor and Survivability

Pros: The main strength of the AMX 50 Foch is its frontal armor. Both the lower (LFP) and upper (UFP) front plates, which cover about 90% of the tank's front, have an effective thickness of 300 mm+.

Cons: There is an optics rangefinder on the roof which has about 200 mm of armor and is almost always a weak spot. However, to deal damage, the enemy must hit the very bottom part of it. This part is also highly vulnerable to HE shells—requiring 7+ kg of TNT for full destruction. Additionally, there is a 20mm autocannon on the roof that is very easily penetrated. Damage from any APHE shell capable of penetrating the rangefinder will often pass through here as well.

Armament

This vehicle features a 120mm gun with two useful shell types:

AP (Solid Shot) — 300 mm penetration: These have strong post-penetration damage for solid rounds. They usually penetrate everything except the extremely tough parts of certain tanks, such as the UFP of a Tiger II or a T-10A. For such targets, penetration is insufficient, but most opponents can be penetrated through the UFP. However, avoid shooting at areas where armor exceeds 270 mm.

HE (High Explosive) — 4.6 kg TNT filler: Carrying these is recommended as they are effective against heavily armored targets like the T95 or T-10A. This shell can easily destroy a tank via overpressure if aimed at weak roof or floor zones.

Additionally, it has a 20mm autocannon that can easily shred lightly armored targets, like the Object 906, even from the front. However, it is nearly useless against aircraft due to poor vertical elevation angles.

Conclusions and Comments

Playing at a distance is recommended. Do not rush too fast in this tank—every action should be thought out due to the machine's specific traits, such as weak sides and a long reload time. You should rely on your good penetration and excellent frontal armor, stay close to allies, and avoid engaging multiple targets at once due to the reload time of approximately 16 seconds. You can push single targets, but it's always preferable to stay with teammates. Never allow any tanks to flank you, as the side armor is extremely weak—less than 60 mm.
