Rank 5 IS-2

Historical Background

The Iosif Stalin IS-2 (Heavy Tank, IS-2) was a Soviet heavy tank of World War II, adopted in 1943 as an evolution of requirements for anti-tank power and protection. Developed from the IS-1/KV designs, it was equipped with the 122mm D-25T rifled gun, which allowed it to effectively engage German heavy tanks and fortifications. Serial production began in 1943–1944; the IS-2 combined powerful frontal armor, a simple construction, and heavy armament, becoming one of the Red Army's key heavy tanks in the final stages of the war.

Quick Overview

Maneuverability

Pros: Possesses adequate mobility for a heavy tank—capable of maintaining the pace of combat on uneven terrain and participating in breakthroughs. It features a reliable running gear and an acceptable top speed for its class in the game.

Cons: Low power-to-weight ratio compared to medium tanks—acceleration is moderate, with high inertia in maneuvers and poor dynamics during sharp course changes. Limited handling on steep slopes and soft ground.

Armor and Survivability

Pros: Massive frontal hull and turret armor—effective against ammunition of the same rank at medium and long distances, especially the central turret sections and the upper glacis plate. Good overall frontal survivability; prone to bouncing direct hits when positioned correctly using the terrain.

Cons: Lower side and rear zones, the roof, and side sections are vulnerable—susceptible to penetration by specialized sub-caliber and HEAT rounds. Weak spots include the gun mantlet, the turret ring joint, and vision devices. Ammunition racks and fuel tanks are vulnerable to side and top hits.

Armament

Main: 122mm D-25T rifled gun. The gun features high armor penetration and extreme destructive power due to its large caliber.

Shells:

Standard APHE: 200mm penetration; 294g TNT equivalent.

Standard HE: 37mm penetration; 3.6kg TNT equivalent.

Cons: Low rate of fire due to the large caliber and two-piece loading design—requires careful calculation of every shot. Limited rapid-fire capability in close quarters reduces the chance of neutralizing multiple targets in quick succession. Aiming capabilities and stabilization are inferior to more maneuverable and modern tanks.

Conclusions and Comments

The IS-2 in War Thunder Mobile is a heavy-hitting brawler for positional and assault gameplay. Its strong frontal armor and 122mm gun make it a formidable opponent in head-on fights and when storming fortifications. It requires patient, thoughtful play: choosing positions wisely, utilizing cover and terrain, and making every shot count due to the slow reload and vulnerable sides. It is optimal in the role of a breakthrough and support tank but vulnerable in mobile duels and against flank attacks.
