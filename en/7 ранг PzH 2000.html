Rank 7 PzH 2000

Historical Background

After the creation of the towed FH70 howitzer, the idea arose to mount it on a tracked chassis to obtain an SPG with better mobility and firing range than the M109. To solve this task, West Germany, the UK, and Italy joined forces in 1973, but the work dragged on for more than a decade. After trials of prototype models revealed low reliability, West Germany withdrew from the program in 1986 and began creating a new SPG from scratch. The design of the PzH 2000 was entrusted to Rheinmetall and Krauss-Maffei Wegmann. The self-propelled unit was required not only to significantly increase its firing range but also to feature high automation, rate of fire, and protection against counter-battery fire. The machine proved successful; after the German Army ordered 185 units in 1996, the PzH 2000 also entered service with several other countries, with a total order volume of 425 machines. The PzH 2000 has been used in the wars in Afghanistan and Ukraine.

Quick Overview

Maneuverability

Pros: Very good mobility for an SPG. The powerful diesel engine (1000 hp) and tracked base from the Leopard 1 allow it to reach speeds of up to 66 km/h forward and 66 km/h backward (reverse gearbox) on roads, enabling quick position changes. Excellent power-to-weight ratio (~17 hp/t).

Cons: Its large dimensions and mass make it cumbersome on soft terrain.

Armor and Survivability

Pros: Bulletproof frontal hull armor (up to ~30 mm at an angle) and the gun mantlet can sometimes protect against aircraft cannons (20-23 mm) and shrapnel.

Cons: Almost a complete lack of protection. The sides (10-20 mm), rear, and roof are penetrated even by heavy machine guns (12.7-14.5 mm). Any incoming HE or HEAT shell is guaranteed to destroy the vehicle. The 5-man crew is spread out across a large casemate.

Armament

Primary: 155mm L/52 howitzer. Its main feature is the rate of fire. It is the second SPG in the game in terms of reload speed, at 5 seconds.

Shells:

M107 (HE): Primary shell. A powerful high-explosive round (approx. 9.14 kg TNT). Destroys light vehicles in one hit; with one or two hits, it can disable even heavy tanks (damaging modules and crew through the roof and gun mantlet).

OE F3 (HE): Effective against lightly armored targets. Explodes with immense force.

M107 (PF) (HE-VT): Effective against aircraft due to its proximity fuse.

Key Features

Targeting Angles: Horizontal guidance is limited to only 11° to the left and right (referring to turret traverse speed). This is the main drawback. To fire in a different direction, you must rotate the entire hull, which is very slow.

Conclusions and Comments

The PzH 2000 is a highly specialized support vehicle and an "artillery punisher." It is not a tank, nor even a tank destroyer in the classical sense. Its strength lies in its ability to rain down a barrage of powerful high-explosive shells on the enemy from a safe distance.
