Rank 9 M60A3 TTS

Historical Background

The M60A3 TTS (abbr. "TTS" — Tank Thermal Sight) is an American medium tank. The M60A3 TTS was an improved model of the earlier M60A3. The gunner received the AN/VSG2 tank thermal sight (TTS), which was the only significant improvement over the M60A3 Passive. 304 M60A3 TTS tanks were newly built (production ended in 1983), 1,391 US Army M60A1 RISE tanks were converted to M60A3 TTS standard by 1990 by the Anniston Army Depot and Mainz Army Depot, and all 748 Army M60A3 tanks were also updated to the TTS standard by 1984.

Quick Review

Mobility

Pros: Acceptable mobility for its mass (≈53 t). The Continental AVDS-1790 diesel engine (750 hp) provides sufficient power-to-weight ratio (~14.4 hp/t) to reach positions. Maximum speed is about 53 km/h.

Cons: Significantly inferior in dynamics compared to contemporaries of its rank. Very slow acceleration, especially on inclines. Cumbersome and sluggish in handling. Poor reverse speed (9 km/h).

Armor and Survivability

Pros: Frontal hull and turret armor have limited resistance against older HEAT shells and small-caliber autocannons. The rounded cast turret can sometimes cause ricochets.

Cons: The armor is outdated and ineffective at its rank. It is easily penetrated by all primary APFSDS and modern HEAT munitions at any distance. Large, flat sides. The layout with 4 crew members and a large ammo rack in the hull makes the tank prone to explosions. The high and narrow profile makes it a prominent "target."

Armament

105mm M68 rifled gun (licensed L7). Two-plane stabilizer. Accurate and reliable.

Ammunition:

M774 (APFSDS): The key shell for this rank. Penetration of ~370mm allows it to fight the frontal armor of most targets (T-62, early T-64/72, T-55AMD-1, etc.) with precise aiming at weak spots.

M456A2 (HEAT-FS): Effective against medium and lightly armored vehicles.

M393A2 (HESH): Effective against lightly armored vehicles.

M735 (APFSDS): Decent dart but with reduced penetration. Ineffective for its rank.

Additionally: 7.62mm and 12.7mm machine guns.

Features

Smoke Grenades (12 launchers). Vertical guidance angles: -10.0° / +20.0°.

Conclusions and Comments

The M60A3 TTS is an "observer" tank and a support sniper. It is not a duelist nor a frontline vanguard. Its role is to deliver precise strikes from a safe distance and play hull-down while more mobile allies create pressure.
