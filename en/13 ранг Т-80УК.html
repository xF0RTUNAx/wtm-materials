Rank 13 T-80UK

Historical Background

The T-80UK is a command variant of the T-80U developed by the Omsk-based KBTM. Visually, it closely resembles the standard "eighty": the only distinct external differences are two antennas and the "Shtora-1" soft-kill active protection system. It was adopted into service in the early 1990s. Key internal differences include the installation of the "Agava-2" thermal imager, an atmospheric parameter sensor, R-163U and R-163K radio stations, the TNA-4 navigation system, a remote detonation system for HE shells, and the AB-1-P28 auxiliary power unit.

Brief Overview

Maneuverability

Pros: Good mobility is provided by the 1250-hp GTD-1250 engine and its high power-to-weight ratio. Thanks to this, the vehicle possesses some of the best dynamic indicators among all MBTs in the game. The T-80UK confidently reaches its top speed on both firm ground and surfaces with low bearing capacity (sand, snow, swamps, etc.).

Cons: However, its reverse speed is insufficient, preventing a quick retreat from a position in case of danger, although it is still quite good compared to the T-72 and T-64 series tanks.

Armor and Survivability

The T-80UK is one of the most protected tanks in its frontal projection in the game. From the front, the tank can only be penetrated by aiming at specific weak spots; shooting at the silhouette will mostly yield no positive results. Turret armor offers even higher resistance.

However, the tank has weak zones: the driver's vision blocks (triplex), the gun mantlet, areas between ERA blocks on the hull and turret, the lower front plate, and the turret ring.

It is also worth noting the weak side hull armor, which is vulnerable even to autocannon fire. Given the weak side armor and excellent frontal protection, you should always face enemy tanks head-on.

Regarding survivability, it is typical for Soviet tanks: the dense layout, low internal volume, and the ammo rack located in the autoloader at the center of the fighting compartment do not favor surviving penetrations.

On the other hand, nothing prevents the tank from bouncing or absorbing a significant number of enemy shells, sometimes with minimal damage.

Additionally: Armor is often most effective in dynamic combat and when forcing the enemy to take more time to aim.

Armament

The tank has up to 28 shells located in the autoloader's "carousel." This allows the tank to maintain a continuous fight using only the autoloader's ammo rack, with a reload speed of 6.5 seconds between shots, which does not depend on the crew's skill level.

The tank commander has an independent duplicate fire control system; meaning, if the gunner is lost, the commander takes over turret aiming and firing. This significantly increases the tank's chances of returning fire without waiting for the commander to change seats.

The turret traverse speed is generally sufficient for comfortable, deliberate play, but it might fall short in dynamic situations when surrounded. Vertical targeting speed is a weak point — when moving over hilly or uneven terrain, the gun might not be able to quickly aim at the enemy.

The tank is equipped with a 12.7-mm NSVT anti-aircraft machine gun and a coaxial 7.62-mm PKT machine gun. These can be used to engage lightly armored open-top vehicles and destroy tracks.

3BM46 "Svinets" — APFSDS. This shell has good post-penetration effects, high armor penetration, and a muzzle velocity of up to 1700 m/s. It can reliably penetrate most opponents when shooting at the silhouette. It is recommended as the primary ammunition type.

Features

It is equipped with the "Shtora-1" soft-kill active protection system. It is designed to disrupt the guidance of certain types of anti-tank guided missiles (ATGMs) by jamming their tracking systems.

Conclusions and Comments

With the addition of its top APFSDS shell, the tank properly shines on the battlefield without artificial restrictions. This vehicle is excellent for fans of high-tier Soviet machinery and dynamic battles with versatile gameplay.
