Rank 13 HSTV-L

Historical Background

The HSTV-L (High Survivability Test Vehicle Lightweight) is an American experimental light tank. In the mid-1970s, the US military initiated a series of studies to develop new tank designs with impressive firepower and survivability. The latter was to be achieved by reducing the vehicle's size rather than increasing its armor. At that time, both the Army and the US Marine Corps needed a replacement for the M551 Sheridan and M50 Ontos tanks. Therefore, a joint research program designated ARMVAL (Advanced Antiarmor Vehicle Evaluation) was launched. It was expected that the prototypes developed under this program would help test various concepts and ideas, which would ultimately lead to the creation of fundamentally new tanks. One of the results of this initiative was the HSTV-L — a project for a compact, lightweight, and maneuverable tank equipped with a 75-mm smoothbore gun featuring an automatic loading mechanism. The HSTV-L was built in 1979 and underwent testing until 1981. In the early 1980s, the Army command deemed the 75-mm gun too weak to destroy modern Soviet MBTs. Furthermore, the US Marine Corps doubted the effectiveness of using the new tank in amphibious landing operations. Consequently, the program was canceled in 1981. However, the vehicle has survived to this day and is currently located in the US Army Armor and Cavalry Collection at Fort Moore.

Brief Overview

Maneuverability

Pros: Excellent metrics in all aspects; we can outrun everyone and be the first to take important positions. Turret traverse and targeting speeds are comparable to an SPAA.

Cons: The vehicle can skid, complicating handling. Massive obstacles can stop it, certain types of terrain and water can reduce practical maneuverability, and collisions and impacts can damage internal modules.

Armor and Survivability

Protection is only sufficient against small-caliber machine guns. Sometimes it might not be destroyed with a single hit due to the considerable amount of empty space inside the tank.

Warning: HEAT and HE shells are lethal. The turret can sometimes withstand hits slightly better.

Armament

Only APFSDS (darts) are of interest. The ammo capacity is small (up to 26 rounds in total). The autoloader works for the entire ammo rack (reload time is 1 second).

It is possible to penetrate NATO tanks from the front by aiming at weak spots, successfully destroy Soviet-design tanks by shooting their sides or angled hulls (hitting the carousel autoloader), and easily destroy enemy guns and mobility modules.

The damage is not high (due to the small caliber) and is inconsistent, but by and large, it is sufficient.

Features

Excellent gun depression/elevation angles and the presence of smoke grenades.

Conclusions and Comments

Recommended only for experienced players. Requires serious, attentive, and rational gameplay to unlock its full potential. Highly dependent on battle conditions.
