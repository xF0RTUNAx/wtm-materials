Rank 13 Merkava Mk.3C

Historical Background

The development of the Merkava Mk 3 tank began in August 1983, effectively before mass production of the Merkava Mk 2 even started. General Israel Tal defined the Mk 3 as a tank of the 1990s, which, in terms of combat effectiveness, had to surpass all main battle tanks of those years: the Leopard 2, Challenger, M1 Abrams, T-80, and T-72. Unlike the Merkava Mk 2, which was essentially an upgraded variant of the Mk 1 tank, the Mk 3 version became virtually a new project. This is clearly visible in the scope of the improvements. Serial production of the Merkava Mk 3 began in April 1990. From the summer of 1990, due to Iraq's invasion of Kuwait, the assembly of new tanks was temporarily suspended, and all efforts were thrown into repairing existing equipment. Serial production resumed in early 1991. In total, over 10 years (1990 - 2000, taking into account the production halt in 1990 - 1991), between 480 and 680 Merkava Mk 3 tanks were built, meaning the production rate was about 50 tanks per year (with the capacity to build at least 120 vehicles per year). The Merkava Mk 3 tanks are used to arm the 188th regular brigade. Production was halted in 2002.

Optical Tracking System (IRST)

The Merkava Mk.3C has the ability to lock onto enemy ATGMs and provide a lead indicator to shoot them down.

  • It locks onto an enemy ATGM in flight (essentially like locking onto an aircraft with radar, displaying a green circle and lead indicator).
