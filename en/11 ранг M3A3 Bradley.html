Rank 11 M3A3 Bradley

Historical Background

The M3A3 "Bradley" is a late modification of the American Bradley Cavalry Fighting Vehicle. Taking into account the experience of the Gulf War in the early 1990s, American engineers created a new modification of the widespread M3 Bradley combat vehicle. The electronic equipment was improved: a new fire control system was introduced, compatible with the inter-vehicle communication system of the M1A2 Abrams tank and the AH-64D Apache Longbow helicopter. The commander received an independent thermal imaging sight, which allows for automatic gun adjustment, automatic target tracking, and tracking multiple targets simultaneously. The M3A3 Bradley modifications began entering US Army units in 1998, and the first vehicles were officially adopted in 2000. By the end of 2010, over 3,300 Bradleys had been upgraded to the A3 version. The vehicles are still in service with the US Army and its allies.

Brief Overview

Maneuverability

Pros: Very good mobility for its weight (≈30 t). The Cummins VTA-903T diesel engine (600 hp) provides a power-to-weight ratio of ~20 hp/t, excellent acceleration, a top speed of about 67 km/h, and reverse speed (~15 km/h). The tracked platform provides good cross-country performance.

Cons: Not as nimble as wheeled reconnaissance vehicles or light tanks. Reverse speed is slow. Neutral steering is reasonably fast, but overall maneuverability is worse than the M3 Bradley due to the increased weight.

Armor and Survivability

Pros: Bulletproof armor with ceramic add-on plates. The front of the hull and turret can withstand fire from 12.7-14.5 mm machine guns and shrapnel.

Cons: Absolutely defenseless against any tank armament, aircraft cannons (23-30+ mm), and ATGMs. The hull is long and tall, making it easy to hit. Although the 3-man crew is spaced out, one lucky HE or APFSDS hit often takes out the entire vehicle.

Armament

Main:

25-mm M242 Bushmaster automatic cannon: Dual-feed system. Choice between:

M919 (APFSDS): ~100 mm of penetration. Used for fighting light armor and the sides of tanks.

M791 (APDS): Effective against lightly armored vehicles and for shooting at the weak spots of enemy MBTs.

ATGMs:

TOW-2A/B ATGM launcher: The main striking force. 12 guided missiles (2 in the launcher + 10 in reserve). TOW-2A is a tandem ATGM, effective against heavy, medium, and light armored vehicles.

TOW-2B — a unique and the most dangerous version. It attacks from above, flying over the target and detonating its tandem warhead downward into the roof. It does not require aiming directly at the turret or hull, and ignores ERA and most active protection systems.

Additionally: 7.62-mm coaxial machine gun.

Features

Stabilizer (works on the move). Smoke grenades (8 pcs). Large vertical guidance angles for the gun (-9°/+59°).

Conclusions and Comments

The M3A3 Bradley is not a scout, but a high-tech tank destroyer and hunter. It is one of the most effective vehicles for fighting heavily armored targets at its own and any higher rank thanks to the unique TOW-2A/B ATGMs.
