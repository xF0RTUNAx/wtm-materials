Rank 6 T29

Historical Background

The T29 was an American heavy tank, the development of which began as a response to the appearance of German heavy machines in Europe: Tiger II, Panther, and Jagdtiger. The US needed a vehicle capable of reliably penetrating their frontal armor while withstanding fire from powerful 88–128 mm guns. February 1944 marked the start of the project. By 1945, the first prototypes were assembled.

The tank weighed about 70 tons, possessed serious frontal protection, and a powerful gun; however, the war ended before the T29 could be brought to serial production. With the disappearance of the "Tiger II" threat, the need for such a machine vanished, and the army shifted focus to new projects—T30, T34, and later the M103.

Today, the T29 exists only in the form of several prototype vehicles used for testing.

Brief Overview

Maneuverability

Pros: Decent turret rotation speed and good vertical guidance angles (gun depression/elevation). Handling is acceptable for a heavy tank.

Cons: Low speed, slow acceleration. Poor hull traverse—light tanks can easily circle you. It is almost impossible to be the first to occupy an important position.

Armor and Survivability

The turret is the main asset of the T29. The turret front is thick, often resulting in "non-penetration" or "hit" notifications. It holds up excellently against hits, except for the large commander's cupola—which is a weak spot. Also vulnerable is a narrow strip above the gun, especially against APHE shells. Occasional penetrations into the gun mantlet are possible due to technological cutouts.

Hull: The front is strong but has two obvious weak spots: the machine gun port and the lower front plate (LFP), which is reliably penetrated by the likes of the Tiger II. Against some opponents, such as the AMX Foch, you can catch ricochets as their shells tend to deflect.

Armament

The T29 is equipped with the 105mm T5E1 gun (NOT 155mm).

Pros: Excellent penetration, high damage per hit, very good gun depression angles, fast aiming, and comfortable firing experience.

Cons: Large muzzle brake—often catches hits, leading to gun damage (crit); long reload time (about 20 seconds)—inferior to the Tiger II with its ~9.7 s.

There are also three M2 Brownings, which allow for: disabling light tank (LT) modules, shooting down air targets, but they often won't manage to kill vital LT crew members in time, so allowing enemies to close the distance is extremely dangerous.

Gameplay Strategy

Playing "hull-down" is the primary style. Given the strong turret and weak hull, the best tactic is to occupy positions where only the turret is visible.

You should only push with teammates. Due to the long reload, you won't be able to quickly trade shots with several targets in a row.

Solo pushing is possible only if: the enemy has weak vehicles, there are only 1–2 of them, and you control the distance.

Do not allow light tanks to get close. They can easily circle you, especially while the gun is on cooldown.

Conclusions and Comments

The T29 allows for aggressive play, but with intelligence. Turret armor provides a sense of confidence—many enemies simply do not know where to penetrate you. The gun is reliable and high-penetrating, allowing it to deal with most targets.

However: the dynamics are poor—you won't be able to drive away or push quickly. The long reload forces you to think ahead. Any mistake in positioning can lead to a fatal hit.

Gameplay: Measured, cautious, but at times very aggressive if the turret is used correctly and you know the enemy's weak zones.
