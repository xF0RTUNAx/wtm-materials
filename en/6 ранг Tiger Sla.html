6 rank Tiger II (Sla.16)

Historical Background

Panzerkampfwagen Tiger ll (Sla.16) was developed as an evolution of the Tiger I. The German command sought to obtain a heavy tank with reinforced frontal armor and a more powerful gun capable of countering new Soviet vehicles with sloped armor.

By the end of 1943, the final design was approved with a 150-mm frontal plate, sloped armor, and a new Henschel turret. Serial production continued until March 1945, with a total of about 489 tanks produced.

In War Thunder Mobile, the Tiger II retains its key features: a strong front, a powerful gun, and vulnerable sides, as well as the need for careful and thoughtful positioning.

Brief Overview

Maneuverability

Pros:

  • Decent straight-line acceleration for a heavy tank.
  • Sufficient speed to take key positions at the beginning of the battle.
  • Relatively comfortable turning on the spot — maneuvering the hull is easier than with most heavies.

Cons:

  • Heavy hull — noticeably loses speed on soft ground, inclines, and when turning.
  • Difficult to evade flanking attacks.
  • When the engine is damaged, mobility drops to almost zero.
  • Large dimensions make it difficult to hide the hull.

Additionally: mobility is sufficient for confident positional play, but not suitable for aggressive pushing or flanking maneuvers.

Armor and Survivability

The hull front is one of the best at its rank, especially with a slight angle (side-scraping). The turret also has good protection if you don't expose the gun mantlet at right angles. It often bounces APHE and weak APCR/APFSDS shells.

The sides are seriously vulnerable, penetrated by almost all tanks. The large silhouette makes it an easy target for artillery and airstrikes. Penetration of the gun mantlet or lower glacis plate (LFP) often leads to critical damage: breech, driver, ammo rack. The double side layer does not save it — if hit at the right angle, penetration with detonation occurs.

Warning: HEAT shells, flanking shots, and top-down attacks are especially dangerous.

Armament

Main gun: 88 mm KwK 43 L/71.

  • Excellent penetration among heavy tanks at its rank.
  • High damage — often destroys the enemy in 1–2 hits.
  • Superb accuracy and firing stability at medium and long ranges.
  • Easily knocks out key modules: engine, transmission, breech, crew.

Reload is not the fastest — misses are critical. Against heavy Soviet equipment, aiming at weak spots is required. In close combat, it is inferior to more maneuverable vehicles.

Practical effect in WTM: the Tiger II (Sla.16) gun guarantees stable damage and duel superiority with proper positioning and the first shot.

Features and Conclusions

The best result is achieved by properly angling the hull. The gameplay shines at medium distances — where the combination of armor and gun is most effective. Requires constant flank control.

It acts confidently in the city if you manage to cover your sides. Good compatibility with team play: supporting and controlling directions increase its impact on the battle.
