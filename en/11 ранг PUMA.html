Rank 11 PUMA

Historical Background

The Schützenpanzer PUMA (short for Panzer Unter Minimalem Aufwand — armored vehicle with minimal cost) is a German infantry fighting vehicle. The PUMA was jointly developed by Krauss-Maffei Wegmann and Rheinmetall in Germany to replace the aging Marder IFVs, which are over 30 years old. Five pre-production vehicles were delivered in 2004. The Puma successfully passed numerous tests, and the first production IFVs were delivered to the Bundeswehr in 2015. Currently, it is the largest procurement program of the German army. The PUMA is gradually replacing the older Marder IFVs and is currently considered the most advanced IFV in the world.

Brief Overview

Maneuverability

Overall, the vehicle feels quite responsive, so you won't have problems with necessary maneuvers in battle. It is also worth noting the fast neutral steering, which allows it to quickly turn around and change position. The mobility of the turret and autocannon can remind you of an SPAAG.

Armor and Survivability

Despite the lack of heavy armor, the Puma demonstrates good survivability. Not without the loss of modules, but with the ability to continue a full fight after repairs. This sometimes happens thanks to the unmanned turret, a sufficient number of crew members, the dimensions, and the layout of the modules.

Additionally: This is best manifested during unsuccessful and thoughtless shots from the enemy. The spacious fighting compartment allows the Puma to withstand fire for a long time, provided the enemy shoots at the side, because most shells will pass straight through without causing serious damage.

Armament

The Puma only has an autocannon to fight against enemies.

It slowly dismantles enemies and requires constant aiming at necessary modules. Tanks are quite difficult to deal with head-on, but there are no problems with destroying modules. The ideal targets are flat sides and lightly armored vehicles.

If we shoot randomly, we risk not being able to take out the enemy in time. At first, you will also have to get used to the rate of fire.

Pay attention to the first-stage ammo stowage so you don't run out of shells at a crucial moment.

Features

It features an electro-optical active protection system (IRCM).

The ammunition is located in the rear of the turret, and in the event of its detonation, blowout panels will save the vehicle from destruction.

Dual fire control system.

Conclusions and Comments

The Puma is well suited for the second half of the battle, especially for destroying groups of enemies who are not focused on us. Try to buy time for an attack through conditional flanking maneuvers or by destroying the enemy's guns and mobility modules. Otherwise, try to stay close to your allies, occupying suitable positions. The vehicle has potential, but unlocking it depends on many factors.
