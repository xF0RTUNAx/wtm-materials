Rank 11 Leopard 2K

Historical Background

The Leopard 2K is the designation for the early prototypes of the German Leopard 2 main battle tank. In the late 1960s, the MBT-70/KPZ-70 joint development program, which involved multiple US and West German defense companies, reached a dead end. The vehicle was not yet finished, the development budget had been exceeded several times over, and the American and German military had vastly different requirements for the tank that couldn't be fulfilled in a single hull. As a result, even before officially exiting this joint program, Germany initiated the "Keiler" (Wild Boar) program to create a completely domestic tank. The first prototype was built and began testing as early as 1969. It was a deep modernization of the Leopard I with new spaced armor, a modified engine, and transmission from the KPz-70. From 1972 to 1974, 16 more different prototypes of the "Wild Boars" were assembled with various turrets and hulls, receiving the collective designation Leopard 2K. They were used to test various armaments, fire control systems (FCS), suspensions, and other vehicle components. One of the turrets, designated T11, stood out from the rest by having a remotely controlled 20-mm autocannon mount, while carrying a 120-mm gun as its main armament. Later, the developments from the Leopard 2K were used in the creation of the pre-production Leopard 2AV versions, which, after modifications in 1979, were officially adopted by the West German army as the Leopard 2.

Brief Overview

Maneuverability

Pros: The MTU MB 873 diesel engine (1500 hp) and relatively moderate weight (~50 t) provide a power-to-weight ratio of ~30 hp/t. Phenomenal acceleration (one of the best in the game), top forward speed of about 79 km/h, and an equal reverse speed of 79 km/h. Excellent maneuverability on rough terrain.

Cons: Due to the rear placement of the transmission, both the engine and transmission are highly vulnerable to hits from behind. It requires careful driving at top speeds.

Armor and Survivability

Pros: "Ceramic/Steel" composite armor on the front of the turret. At its optimal combat distance (1-1.5 km), it can withstand hits from early APFSDS and old 105-mm shells. Excellent multi-layered armor on the upper glacis plate. The upper front plate (UFP) provides a good level of protection for its rank. Spacious layout with 4 crew members.

Cons: The "cheeks" of the turret (between the gun mantlet and the main armor) are a critical weakness. They consist of thin steel and can be penetrated by almost anything. The sides and rear, like on all "Leopards", are practically paper-thin. The large turret and high profile make the tank a conspicuous target.

Armament

Main gun: 120-mm Rh120-L/44 smoothbore gun. An iconic next-generation weapon with excellent ballistics.

Shells:

DM12 (HEAT-FS): A very powerful high-explosive anti-tank shell with a tandem warhead. It destroys IFVs, APCs, and SPAAGs with a single hit anywhere. It deals critical damage even to heavy tanks when hitting vulnerable zones (sides, rear, turret), often taking out multiple crew members or modules.

DM13 (APFSDS): The base sub-caliber shell. Sufficient to defeat most targets head-on at its rank.

DM23 (APFSDS): The best shell available for the Leopard 2K. Excellent armor penetration (410mm), which is enough to disable any peer vehicle.

Additionally: A 20-mm Rh202 automatic cannon mounted coaxially with the main gun. It can be used against lightly armored targets, aircraft, or to shoot down incoming ATGMs. There is also a 7.62-mm machine gun.

Features

Excellent gun depression and elevation angles (-9/+20). Equipped with smoke grenades (8 pcs).

Conclusions and Comments

The Leopard 2K is a unique hybrid of a high-speed tank destroyer and an assault vehicle. It is neither a classic "ambush" Leopard 1 nor a heavily armored sniper like the later "Leopard 2" models. It is an aggressive duelist and a chameleon, easily switching ammunition types to suit the situation.
