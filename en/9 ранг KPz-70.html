Rank 9 KPz-70

Historical Background

The KPz-70 was a joint American-German main battle tank project. The goal was to create a unified vehicle that could replace the aging M60 and Leopard I while meeting modern requirements. Work began in 1963 and continued throughout the 1960s. The first prototype of the KPz-70 was presented in October 1967. Unlike the American MBT-70, the German-built machines featured a Daimler-Benz MB 873 KA-500 engine, an MG3 coaxial machine gun, and other minor adjustments. For their time, these were highly ambitious and advanced machines, incorporating numerous innovative solutions like adjustable hydropneumatic suspension, composite armor, a multi-purpose main gun, and advanced electronics (FCS, guidance devices, navigation). Trials began in early 1968, highlighting the tank's superior mobility, excellent firepower, and modern digital control systems. However, the project faced many problems, including different conceptual visions and coordination difficulties between US and German specialists. By 1969, costs had exceeded initial forecasts fivefold, while the vehicle still required significant refinement. Consequently, Germany withdrew in 1970 to develop its own tank, which later became the Leopard 2. In total, 14 experimental MBT-70/KPz-70 units were built, 7 by each side.

Brief Overview

Mobility

Pros: Outstanding, especially for a 50-ton tank. The 1500 hp engine and hydropneumatic suspension provide a high power-to-weight ratio (~29 hp/t). Excellent acceleration with a top speed of ~70 km/h in both forward and reverse.

Cons: High inertia due to its mass; it loses significant speed during sharp turns.

Armor and Survivability

Pros: Very strong composite armor on the turret front and upper glacis plate (UFP). Low-profile, sleek "capsule" turret design.

Cons: A massive weakness is the lower glacis plate (LFP). It is thin, practically unprotected, and impossible to hide due to the hull's unique shape. The sides and rear are paper-thin. The crew consists of only 3 members (driver in the hull, commander and gunner in the turret), which makes any penetration highly critical.

Armament

Primary: 152mm XM150E5 gun-launcher. Capable of firing both conventional shells and ATGMs.

Ammunition:

XM578E1 (APFSDS): A secondary shell, best used after exhausting ATGMs. Good penetration but low muzzle velocity and noticeable drop-off at long ranges.

MGM-51C (ATGM): Guided missile (manual wire guidance). Good penetration (~430 mm), allowing it to defeat almost any target.

M409A1 (HEAT): Effective against light, medium, and heavy armor for disabling MBT modules or crew members.

Additionally: A 20mm Rh202 automatic cannon in a separate remote-controlled mount on the turret. It can be used against light targets and aircraft independently of the main gun.

Features

Autoloader: 6-second reload rate, independent of crew health. Excellent vertical guidance angles: -10°/+20°. Equipped with smoke grenades. Limited number of ATGMs (6 total).

Conclusions and Comments

The KPz-70 is a technological demonstrator and a tank for experienced duelists. It is a machine with outstanding characteristics (armor, mobility, armament) but fatal vulnerabilities (LFP, only 3 crew members). It requires perfect map knowledge and precise positioning to succeed.
