Rank 13 Merkava Mk.3B

Historical Background

The Merkava Mk.3 is a modification of the Israeli Merkava Mk.2 main battle tank, developed in the late 1980s. The vehicle received a new suspension system, a 1200 hp engine along with a new transmission, and a new 120-mm gun developed by Israel Military Industries based on the German L44. Mass production of the tank began in 1990.

Parallel to mass production, the tank's design was constantly being improved. Thus, in 1994, the Merkava Mk.3B modification was created, featuring reinforced turret armor, hull design refinements, and other changes. In total, until production ended in 2002, about 700 Merkava Mk.3 tanks were produced in various versions, which are still in service with the IDF.

Brief Overview

Maneuverability

Pros: Small maps and the tank's good responsiveness somewhat hide its shortcomings.

Cons: Maneuverability metrics are quite mediocre for its rank, meaning you will often be one of the last to reach key positions.

On the other hand, the turret traverse and gun targeting speeds are practically very optimal and comfortable, especially if you have upgraded the corresponding crew skills.

Armor and Survivability

Due to its large silhouette, the tank is a good target for peer vehicles and can be penetrated almost anywhere.

Engineering features sometimes lead to destruction from a single lucky hit. But more often, 2 or more hits will be required for elimination. Particular situations play in our favor and allow us to survive longer, though most likely without the ability to return fire, resulting in a large expenditure of repair resources and a lengthy repair time itself.

The turret will not particularly save you from penetrations either, but it is still a priority in terms of playstyle. Due to its specific shape, there is a chance to catch a non-penetration or a ricochet. In case of taking damage, it will not be as significant as hitting the hull and will allow you to continue the battle with minimal losses.

Warning: The huge sides are even more painful and effectively take damage even from autocannons. In principle, sidescraping or diamond angling is contraindicated for this tank.

Armament

A top APFSDS shell (dart) with excellent penetration, which definitely becomes the primary shell.

Quite often it deals good post-penetration damage, but there are moments when the enemy is barely damaged.

Reload time is on par with Leopards, which is a normal indicator for the rank, but not desirable for most duels.

Features

A huge amount of smoke grenades. Dual fire control system.

Take into account the forward placement of mobility modules and the rear placement of the turret when playing from cover.

Also note that the vertical guidance angles (gun depression) are not as outstanding compared to NATO counterparts.

Conclusions and Comments

We get something similar to an Abrams, but in a different form factor. Technical features require unique adaptation to the course of the battle. It is recommended to minimize the probability of taking damage and play as a support for your allies, because otherwise, a mistake will hardly be forgiven. Positional play involves hiding the bulky hull and showing only the turret. A highly skill-dependent and specific tank that will suit experienced players, collectors, and fans of this kind of gameplay. For most players, it is recommended to look towards American or other effective and versatile vehicles.
