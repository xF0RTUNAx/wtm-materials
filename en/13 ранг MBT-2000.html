Rank 13 MBT-2000

Historical Background

The MBT-2000/"Al-Khalid" (VT-1A) is a jointly developed Sino-Pakistani main battle tank. The development of the MBT-2000 was initiated by Pakistan in 1989. At that time, the Pakistani army needed a replacement for its outdated T-55s and Type 69s, which could no longer withstand the new tanks entering service in other countries. As a basis for it, Heavy Industries Taxila, in cooperation with the Chinese company Norinco, chose the Type 90-IIM tank and heavily modernized it, replacing the gun, fire control system, and the cooling and air filtration designs. The harsh climate of Pakistan forced the designers to look for a new power plant, and after a series of tests, the Ukrainian 6TD-2 diesel engine was selected for the future vehicle. The first prototype of the MBT-2000 was presented in 1991, but the first production tanks under the designation "Al-Khalid" entered service with the Pakistani army only in 2000. Today, the MBT-2000 is the main battle tank of Pakistan, and it has also been exported to several other countries.

Brief Overview

Maneuverability

The main drawback in the maneuverability of Soviet top-tier tanks, represented by a slow reverse speed, was eliminated by installing a new powerful engine and a stepped gearbox. The Chinese tank boasts good metrics for power-to-weight ratio, top speed, and acceleration. Also, the vehicle can excellently turn on the spot (neutral steering), allowing it to generally be on par with the most mobile peer vehicles. You can be one of the first to reach key positions, effectively retreat, and execute various tactics thanks to its pleasant maneuverability.

Armor and Survivability

Protection and survivability are based on the Soviet philosophy of tank building. The Chinese analog of explosive reactive armor (ERA) has equivalent metrics compared to Kontakt-5, but with an improved layout density. In turn, the main armor includes Western elements. In principle, the key vulnerable zones are identical to the Soviet ones, but due to the combination of technologies, some of them have additionally armored areas. The sides remain a very weak spot, vulnerable to effective attacks even from autocannons. The armor works more reliably against older APFSDS (darts), while the latest variants can more freely penetrate almost all zones. The turret sometimes allows you to catch non-penetrations or take insignificant damage. It is not uncommon to survive several hits and anti-tank missile attacks. Ultimately, you never know if you will be taken out with a single shot or get off with minor damage.

Additionally: The armor is often realized in dynamic movement and by forcing the enemy to spend more time aiming.

Armament

The top APFSDS shells will please you with optimal penetration and good damage.

The only key disadvantage is the long reload time. On the other hand, the metrics and execution do not depend on the crew (it uses a Soviet-style autoloader).

Unlike its Soviet counterparts, the Chinese tank features improved turret traverse and horizontal targeting speeds.

The vertical guidance angles (gun depression) are still uninspiring, but this drawback is not as noticeable on our small maps.

Features

Note that the vertical guidance angles have different values depending on the hull's position due to engineering specifics.

Conclusions and Comments

A vehicle with a Soviet gameplay philosophy but with some noticeably improved metrics. Thus, this tank is primarily recommended for fans of Soviet tanks, as they receive an additional, improved top-tier vehicle for regular play. The Chinese tank can also suit beginners, occasionally allowing them to get away with mistakes without depriving them of maneuverability options. Certainly, the vehicle is not the ultimate weapon, but it truly shines among its fans.
