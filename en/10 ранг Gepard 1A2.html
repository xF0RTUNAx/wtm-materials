Rank 10 Gepard 1A2

Historical Background

The Gepard 1A2 is a German self-propelled anti-aircraft gun based on the Leopard 1 tank chassis, developed in the 1960s–1970s as a mobile short-range air defense system to protect mechanized units from low-flying targets. It is equipped with a twin-gun turret housing two 35 mm automatic cannons and a radar system for target detection and tracking. In the 1A2 variant, the electronics and ammunition were improved compared to earlier modifications, making the vehicle an effective tactical air defense platform.

Brief Overview

Maneuverability

Pros: Good mobility thanks to the Leopard 1 chassis — high top speed and decent maneuverability on roads and solid ground; allows for quickly taking positions and changing firing locations.

Cons: Large dimensions and weight (tank chassis) make maneuvers in tight urban environments difficult; the running gear and suspension are not designed for extreme off-road speeds.

Armor and Survivability

Pros: The reliability of the Leopard 1 chassis provides basic protection and mobility. The crew and component compartments are designed with field combat survivability in mind.

Cons: Almost entirely 'light' protection — the armor is only designed to protect against shrapnel and small arms; vulnerable to ATGMs, tank guns, and aviation. The tall turret and open firing cabin make the vehicle conspicuous and vulnerable to hits.

Armament

Main gun: 2 x 35 mm automatic cannons (Oerlikon/KDA variants) in a twin-gun turret, stabilized and equipped with a semi-automated tracking system.

Shells:

Uses 35×228 mm ammunition variants (e.g., high-explosive, high-explosive fragmentation, and armor-piercing incendiary/fragmentation incendiary types) with a high combined rate of fire from both guns, providing a dense curtain of fire against aircraft and light armored vehicles.

Features:

Radar detection and target tracking channel, allowing for effective detection and aiming at low-flying targets; 360° turret rotation and high gun elevation angle.

Features

The presence of Stinger missiles with radar guidance creates the biggest difference from its predecessor. Performs excellently in defensive tasks and providing air cover; weak against direct hits from armored vehicles and anti-tank weapons; requires cover.

Conclusions and Comments

The Gepard 1A2 in-game specializes in short-range air defense: its high rate of fire, radar, and ability to quickly react to aerial threats make it indispensable for protecting your forces. At the same time, the vehicle is vulnerable in duels with armored vehicles and ATGMs, so it requires smart positioning, cover, and the use of local concealment. But it also has missiles that are highly effective against aircraft. Moreover, thanks to the APDS belt with 120mm of penetration, it is effective against medium armor even at high ranks. The best missile SPAAG at the time of publication.
